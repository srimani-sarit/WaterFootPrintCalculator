How to Save Water

Water conservation is crucial for a sustainable future. Click on each tip to learn more:

Reducing shower time by just a few minutes can save gallons of water. Using water-efficient showerheads can also reduce consumption.
A small drip can waste up to 20 gallons of water per day. Regularly check and repair leaks in faucets, pipes, and toilets.
Using rain barrels can help collect and reuse rainwater for irrigation, reducing dependency on municipal water supplies.
Running dishwashers and washing machines only when full maximizes efficiency and minimizes water waste.
Water plants in the early morning or late evening to reduce evaporation. Drip irrigation systems can help minimize waste.
When washing your car or watering plants, using a bucket instead of a hose can save a significant amount of water.
Upgrading to low-flow or dual-flush toilets can reduce household water use by thousands of gallons per year.
Leaving the tap running while brushing can waste up to 4 gallons of water per minute. Turn it off to conserve water.
