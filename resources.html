SDG 6 - Water Resources

SDG 6 aims to ensure the availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.

Click on each resource to learn more:

Oceans cover about 71% of Earth's surface and are the largest water resource, though they contain saltwater.
Rivers and streams provide freshwater for drinking, agriculture, and industry.
Glaciers and ice caps store the majority of the world's freshwater but are melting due to climate change.
Groundwater is stored in aquifers underground and is a vital source of drinking water.
Wetlands act as natural water filters and provide habitat for diverse species.
Water exists in the atmosphere as vapor and contributes to the water cycle through precipitation.
Lakes and reservoirs store freshwater for human consumption and irrigation.
Rainwater harvesting is a sustainable method of collecting and storing rain for later use.
